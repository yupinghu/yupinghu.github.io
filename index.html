Webpages like this are an anachronism these days, supplanted by social media and such:

  • Google+
  • LinkedIn
  • Resume

I used to be a big believer in the power of the web and the internet to help people form communities, but then I realized that the ad-supported model subverts the incentives of the various sites and that the term "social media" is only half right. So despite it being the first link above, I don't actually use Google+ (and certainly not Facebook) but it's still a handy way to find me and get some info about me. The other two links are only there because I have to feed my family so it kinda matters to keep those up to date.

It'd be nice if, by the time my kids are old enough to use the web, my old naive hopes about how the internet can be used to bring people together in a meaningful way come to pass. It'd be nicer if I contributed to that vision in some small way.

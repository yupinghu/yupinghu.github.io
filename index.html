Webpages like this are an anachronism these days, supplanted by social media and such. I used to be a big believer in the power of the internet to help people form communities, but recent history taught us that the ad-supported business model of much of Big Tech leads to problematic and evil behaviors for those companies. At the same time, the term "social media" is at best half right: it's pretty isolating. It'd be nice if, by the time my kids are old enough to use the web, my old naive hopes about how the internet can be used to bring people together in a meaningful way come to pass. It'd be nicer if I contributed to that vision in some small way.
